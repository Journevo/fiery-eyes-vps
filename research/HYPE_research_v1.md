## 13. RISKS (ranked by severity)

### 1. Token unlock dilution (HIGH)
76% of supply still locked. Core contributor vesting releases ~10M HYPE/month through Nov 2027. If team sells aggressively, buyback cannot absorb all supply. FDV ($36.5B) is 4x circulating cap ($9B). Mitigant: Team has been transparent and modest in selling so far. Buyback fund absorbed Nov 2025 unlock with ease.

### 2. Smart contract / exploit risk (HIGH)
Young protocol. Fully on-chain order book + margin system = complex attack surface. No major exploit to date, but it hasn't been battle-tested through a full cycle. A Wormhole-style exploit ($325M) would be devastating. Mitigant: HFT team has deep security expertise. Protocol runs on own L1 (smaller attack surface than EVM apps).

### 3. Regulatory action (MEDIUM-HIGH)
Leveraged perps without KYC = regulatory target. CFTC could theoretically go after protocol participants. Mitigant: Decentralised protocol is harder to shut down than a company. But regulatory uncertainty suppresses institutional adoption.

### 4. Revenue cyclicality (MEDIUM)
Trading volume is cyclical. In a deep bear market, volume drops 70-80%. Revenue would drop proportionally, weakening the buyback flywheel. Mitigant: Product diversification (RWA, prediction markets, spot) reduces dependence on crypto perps alone.

### 5. Competition (MEDIUM)
Lighter, Aster, GMX V2, dYdX all competing for DeFi derivatives volume. Mitigant: >70% market share and the flywheel create structural advantages. Network effects in derivatives are strong.

### 6. Validator centralisation (LOW-MEDIUM)
Small validator set relative to ETH/SOL. If a few validators collude or go offline, it could disrupt the network. Mitigant: Team is aware. Validator set growing. HyperBFT designed for high-performance validation.
