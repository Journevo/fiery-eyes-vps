## 4. FUNDAMENTAL ANALYSIS

### Revenue & Fees
This is where RENDER gets complicated:
- **Network processes real work:** 68M+ frames, studios using it, AI labs using Dispersed
- **But token revenue capture is indirect:** Users pay in fiat. The BME model burns RENDER equivalent to the fiat value of jobs completed. New RENDER is minted to reward node operators.
- **Net deflationary IF:** Burn rate > mint rate (depends on usage growth outpacing emission schedule)
- **2025 emissions:** 5,637,150 RENDER tokens emitted (split between network operations and foundation)
- **No direct fee-to-token-holder mechanism** like HYPE's 97% buyback. The value capture is through supply reduction (burns), not revenue distribution.

## 5. TOKENOMICS & SUPPLY

### Supply Structure
- **Max supply:** 644,168,762 RENDER (hard cap)
- **Total supply:** ~533.5M RENDER
- **Circulating supply:** ~520M RENDER (81% of max unlocked)
- **Remaining locked/TBD:** ~235M RENDER

### Burn-and-Mint Equilibrium (BME) — RNP-001
This is Render's core tokenomic mechanism:
1. **User submits rendering job** (pays in fiat)
2. **Equivalent RENDER value is BURNED** from supply
3. **Node operator receives NEWLY MINTED RENDER** as payment
4. **If burn > mint:** Supply shrinks → deflationary → price should rise
5. **If mint > burn:** Supply grows → inflationary → dilution

### Emission Data (2025 Annual Report)
- **2025 total emissions:** 5,637,150 RENDER
- **Split between:** Network operations and Foundation activities
- **This is LOW** relative to total supply (~1% annual emission rate)

### Key Tokenomics Facts
- **81% already unlocked:** Unlike HYPE (24%), most RENDER is already in circulation. Less dilution risk ahead.
- **Hard cap exists:** 644M max. Unlike SOL (no cap), there IS a ceiling.
- **BME burns reduce supply** with every rendering job completed
- **21Shares RENDER ETP** listed (Nov 2024) — institutional access exists
- **Valour RENDER ETP** also listed (Dec 2024)

## 13. RISKS (ranked by severity)

### 1. AI narrative rotation (HIGH)
RENDER's price is driven by AI sector sentiment, not its own fundamentals. If the AI narrative cools (like the metaverse narrative did in 2022), RENDER could remain depressed for years even while the network grows. Mitigant: Unlike "metaverse" tokens, AI has real enterprise adoption with measurable spending ($826B by 2030).

### 2. Competition from centralised cloud (HIGH)
AWS, GCP, Azure are building massive AI compute capacity. If they drop prices, decentralised compute's cost advantage erodes. Mitigant: Render offers 1/10th cost, no waitlists, and fiat pricing. Also addresses different market (small-medium studios, not just hyperscalers).

### 3. Indirect token value capture (MEDIUM-HIGH)
BME burns RENDER, but there's no direct revenue share or dividend-like mechanism. Token value depends entirely on burn rate exceeding mint rate. If usage doesn't grow fast enough, the model is inflationary. Mitigant: 2025 emissions were only ~1% of supply. Low emission rate means even modest usage keeps it near-neutral.

### 4. Competition from decentralised alternatives (MEDIUM)
io.net, Akash, Nosana all competing in the same space. io.net is also on Solana and growing aggressively. Mitigant: Render has 8 years of production history, 68M frames, Hollywood studio validation. Competitors are earlier and less proven.

### 5. Security incidents (MEDIUM)
Polygon incident (Jul 2025) was a warning. Any compromise of the rendering pipeline (where sensitive studio assets are processed) would damage trust. Mitigant: End-to-end encryption, Solana migration removed Polygon risk.

### 6. -86% drawdown recovery challenge (LOW-MEDIUM)
